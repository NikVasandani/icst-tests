How this test works:

In each trial you will see a 3D geometric object (such as a cube, pyramid, sphere or flat surface) with a smaller floating object attached to one of its faces.

The floating object has been tilted away from the correct position. Your task is to adjust the floating object so that it sits perpendicular (at a right angle) to the surface it is resting on.

Controls:

Up & Down Arrow — tilt the floating object (coarse adjustment)
'R' & 'F' keys — tilt the floating object (fine adjustment)
'Enter' — confirm your answer and move to the next trial
'ESC' — end the test early

The Practice mode gives you 25 trials with live feedback showing the current angle and your accumulated error. The full Test has 35 trials with no angle feedback — you must judge perpendicularity by eye alone.

We recommend completing a practice round first to familiarise yourself with the controls before attempting the full test.
