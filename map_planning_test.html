Map Planning Test

Instructions

This is a test of your ability to find the shortest route between two places as quickly as possible. The map shows a city grid where:

  • Dark lines are streets you can travel along. Circles are road-blocks — you cannot pass through them. Numbered squares are buildings.
  • Find the shortest route between two lettered points on the map perimeter. Your answer is the number on the building that your route passes.
  • The shortest route will always pass along the side of one and only one numbered building.
  • A building is not considered passed if a route only touches a corner and not a full side.
  • The same numbered building may appear as the answer for more than one route.

Your score is the number of correct answers. It will not be to your advantage to guess unless you have some idea which route is correct. Work as rapidly as you can without sacrificing accuracy.
